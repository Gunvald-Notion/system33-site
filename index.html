Galdr — Mathematical Language of the Void

Coming soon

Traditional math education routes through sequential symbol processing — read the formula, memorize the steps, repeat. Galdr takes a different path: enter a mental spatial environment, speak mathematical notation as commands, and feel the forces reshape the space around you. Instead of reading about what Σ means, you stand inside it and feel accumulation pull.


The method is grounded in research. Dyslexic brains show a measurable advantage in holistic spatial visualization (Haskins Labs, Yale). Spatial reasoning is a proven predictor of physics and math problem-solving (Kozhevnikov, 2007). And embodied cognition research shows that mathematical understanding is built on physical experience, not abstract symbols (Nature, 2022). Galdr connects these findings into a single method — routing mathematics through the spatial channel where the brain is fastest, instead of the sequential channel where it struggles.

About

System 33 started as a personal problem. I have dyslexia — every piece of knowledge I gain costs more effort than it costs most people. Losing it after all that work was something I couldn't accept. So I built methods that make things stick. Not through memorization, but through understanding where things come from and why they exist.


What started as a learning tool grew into an operating system for thinking — a collection of methods I use daily for learning, making decisions, and building things. System 33 is the public version. Every method is free and open source.
