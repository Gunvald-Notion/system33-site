Enkel & Multippel Regresjon

Energy converges at the center. Each predictor pulls from a direction. The score takes shape from the sum of all forces resolved at the convergence point.

First principle. A regression model is not a surface in a cloud. It is a convergence of forces. The intercept b̂₀ is the equilibrium — where the energy sits when no predictor fires. Every predictor Xⱼ is an arm of force radiating outward; its coefficient b̂ⱼ is the strength of pull per unit of input. When a predictor takes a value, its arm contributes b̂ⱼ·xⱼ — positive pulls the center toward its direction, negative pulls it back. The final position of the energy is ŷ — the predicted score. Every other rune in Kap 11 is a way of reading this same convergence from a different angle.

Formula Reference — Formelark Notation

Canonical formulas from the eksamen formelark. Use these symbols verbatim — b̂ not β̂, t_TS not t_obs, df = n − k − 1 for multippel regresjon.

Enkel lineær regresjonKap 11.1
Yⱼ = b₀ + b₁·Xⱼ + εⱼ
One predictor, one arm. The simplest convergence.
Multippel regresjonKap 11.8
Yⱼ = b₀ + b₁·X₁ⱼ + b₂·X₂ⱼ + ... + bₖ·Xₖⱼ + εⱼ
k arms, k forces. Each coefficient holds its meaning when all others are held still.
Testverdi (generelt)Kap 9 + 11
t_TS = (utvalgsverdi − hypoteseverdi) / standardfeil
= (θ̂ − θ₀) / se(θ̂)
Force measured in units of its own uncertainty. df = n − k − 1 for multippel regresjon. For coefficient signifikance, θ₀ = 0.
R² & Justert R²Kap 11.3
R² = SSR / SST = 1 − SSE / SST
R²_just = 1 − (1−R²)(n−1) / (n−k−1)
Fraction of Y-variance the convergence catches. Justert R² penalizes extra arms that don't earn their keep.
F-statistic (hele modellen)Kap 11.5
F = MSR / MSE
= (forklart varians / k) / (uforklart varians / (n−k−1))
df = (k, n−k−1)
Whole-model test. H₀: all bⱼ = 0. Reject when F > F_CV at chosen α.
RSE — Residual standardfeilKap 11.3
RSE = √(Σeᵢ² / (n−k−1))
Average gap between data points and the convergence. Lower RSE means tighter convergence.
VIF — Variance Inflation FactorKap 11.8
VIFⱼ = 1 / (1 − R²ⱼ)
Multikollinearitet diagnostic. VIF > 5–10 indicates the arms are pulling in nearly the same direction — coefficients become unstable.
Konfidensintervall for b̂ⱼKap 11.5
b̂ⱼ ± t_(α/2, n−k−1) · se(b̂ⱼ)
Range where the true bⱼ likely sits, given the sample.
