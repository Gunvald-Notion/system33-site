Galdr Spell · Kap 3

Drainage

A pool of N. Draw S. The pool shrinks with every pick. Count the ways — ordered or unordered, draining or refilling.

The pool shrinks. Every pick removes one item from the pool. The next pick has fewer to choose from. The product of the shrinking choices is the count of distinct ways.

When Drainage Feeds the Probability House

When the problem asks for a probability — not just a count — you do two drainage walks:

Mulige — total ways. This is the denominator.

Gunstige — ways that satisfy the condition. This is the numerator.

P = gunstige / mulige

Drainage counts the ways. Laplace turns them into mass. The Probability House takes over for composition.

Formula Reference — Kap 3
n! Full drainage
n! = n × (n−1) × (n−2) × … × 2 × 1
Every item leaves the pool, one at a time. The total number of ways to empty the entire pool. nPr and nCr are built from n!.
nPr — Permutations Ordered drainage
nPr = N! / (N−S)! = N × (N−1) × … × (N−S+1)
S items leave a pool of N. Order matters. The (N−S)! on the bottom cancels the unused rounds. What survives is the S rounds you actually walked.
nCr — Combinations Unordered drainage
nCr = N! / (S! × (N−S)!) = nPr / S!
Same drainage as nPr, but order collapsed. Divide by S! to strip the costume-count — how many ways S items can be rearranged among themselves.
NS No drainage
NS = N × N × N × … (S times)
Pool refills after each pick. Every round has the same N choices. No shrinking. The non-draining cousin.
